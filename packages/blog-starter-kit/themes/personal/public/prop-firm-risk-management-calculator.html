Prop Firm Risk Management Calculator

Always trade according to what you’re willing to lose — not what you hope to gain.

If you’ve ever wondered whether to trade NQ or MNQ, or how many contracts you can safely manage, this calculator is for you. This will help you understand risk management so you can develop the proper mindset.

Before you begin: make sure you understand the drawdown rules for your prop firm account. Once you know those rules, enter:

  • Available drawdown for your account
  • Risk tolerance (percent of drawdown you’ll risk per trade)
  • Dollars per tick (defaults to $5 for NQ)

The calculator will instantly show how many contracts — and which instrument — you can safely trade within your limits.

Why this works — the “five losses in the bank” rule:

Always keep five full losses in the bank. That means you should risk no more than 20% of your drawdown on any single trade. If you hit five losses in a row, you’ll have exhausted your drawdown.

  • Example inputs: Drawdown = $1,000, Stop = 100 ticks, Tick = $5 (NQ).
  • One NQ contract risk = 100 × $5 = $500.
  • 20% of drawdown = $200. So $200 ÷ $500 = 0.4 NQ → trade 4 MNQ.

This method keeps you disciplined, consistent, and in the game — even after a string of losses.
